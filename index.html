🎯 Step 1: Find Your Tempo

Begin with 30/10 and work through the options.

Tap play, grab a club, and sync your swing to the notes:

🎵 First note = takeaway

🎵 Second note = top of backswing

🎵 Third note = impact

Test each tempo—some will feel off, others closer. Exploring them all helps you discover your best rhythm.

🏌️ Step 2: Train with It

Once you've chosen a tempo, stick with it.

It may feel awkward at first, but that's part of the process—consistency is key.

Missing the "impact" note is common. Often it's due to too much upper-body movement or holding lag too long. With steady practice, your timing will click into place.

📅 Step 3: Build a Routine

A good baseline: 5–10 minutes a day.

Range routine: 3 swings without a ball → 1 swing with a ball.

Switch clubs often to recreate on-course variety.

Remember: tempo training is part of practice, not the whole session.

💡 Extra Tips

🎧 Use earbuds when possible.

⛳ Warm up with the trainer before playing.

🗣️ Repeat the notes in your head (or out loud) to keep rhythm on the course.

🌟 Final Note

Golf is tough, but tempo training makes it smoother. Stay patient, stay positive—your swing will thank you.
